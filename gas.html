Gaseous Planet Composition

The gaseous planets (or rather, Jovian planets) are made up mostly of hydrogen and helium, with oxygen being mostly bonded with hydrogen to form water. There are also small traces of gases such as ammonia and methane. The core of these planets are not known for sure, though it's speculated these are made of solid rock, metals and ice.
The planets farther from the sun in our solar system are gas giants for reasons stated in the Terrestrial Planet section (gravitational attraction and stellar winds). They did not have as much of an issue with stellar winds and so therefore were able to reach and keep a larger size.

A related planet type is ice giant, which is the other subset of Jovian planets. These are similar to gas giants, being farther away from the sun and having a lack of surface.
